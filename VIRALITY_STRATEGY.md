# "While You Were Deciding" -- Virality Strategy
## From Interesting to Irresistible: Making the Card Urgently Shareable

---

## 1. THE THREE PSYCHOLOGICAL TRIGGERS (Spotify Wrapped Mapping)

Spotify Wrapped gets shared 60M+ times because it activates three triggers simultaneously. Here is how each maps to "While You Were Deciding" -- and what is currently missing.

### Trigger 1: IDENTITY MIRROR -- "This says something about ME"

**Spotify Wrapped**: Your top artist says you are a specific kind of person. The data becomes a personality statement. People share it because it is a proxy for "here is who I am."

**Our card currently**: Shows what the universe was doing on a date. That is interesting -- but it is about the UNIVERSE, not about the USER. The decision text is the only personal element, and it is buried as context.

**What is missing**: The card needs to feel like a personality reveal. The decision IS the identity signal. Someone who quit their job on the day a 7.2 earthquake hit Japan is telling you something about themselves. The card needs to frame the DECIDER as the protagonist, not the cosmos.

**Fix**: Reframe the card hierarchy. The decision should be the hero. The cosmic events are the supporting cast that makes the decision feel dramatic. Add a line like:

```
WHILE 4.2 BILLION PEOPLE WENT ABOUT THEIR DAY
YOU DECIDED TO [quit your job / move to Berlin / say yes]
```

This makes the sharer the main character. That is the unlock.

### Trigger 2: SURPRISING DATA -- "I did not know that about myself / my moment"

**Spotify Wrapped**: "You were in the top 0.5% of Radiohead listeners." The specificity creates surprise, and surprise creates screenshots.

**Our card currently**: Shows headlines and space data. These are factual but lack the "wait, really?" moment. There is no stat that makes someone gasp.

**What is missing**: A single surprising, specific number tied to THEIR date. Not generic -- hyper-specific.

**Fix**: Add one or two "cosmic coincidence" stats to the card. Examples:
- "While you were deciding, the ISS completed 4.7 orbits overhead"
- "3 earthquakes shook the planet in the 24 hours around your decision"
- "An asteroid passed Earth at 14,200 mph -- closer than the Moon"
- "The Earth traveled 1.6 million miles through space that day"

The key: ONE big number, presented dramatically. This is the "screenshot stat" -- the thing that makes someone say "look at this."

### Trigger 3: SOCIAL PROOF / FOMO -- "Everyone is doing this and I need mine"

**Spotify Wrapped**: Floods every feed simultaneously. You see 20 friends post theirs. You HAVE to get yours.

**Our card currently**: No mechanism for this. A shared card looks like a one-off image. There is no signal that this is a THING that other people are also doing.

**What is missing**: The card itself must communicate "this is a tool you can use too" -- without being an ad.

**Fix**: Add a subtle but unmistakable URL/watermark at the bottom of the card:
```
whileyouweredeciding.com
```
Plus: The share text (see Section 3) must create curiosity in viewers, not just expression for sharers. When viewers see 3-4 friends post these cards in a week, the FOMO kicks in.

## 2. CARD DESIGN TWEAKS (Keeping the Aesthetic)

The dark background + monospace + "DECIDED" stamp is strong. Here are small additions that increase screenshot-worthiness across platforms.

### A. Add a "MISSION DATE" header with dramatic formatting

Current: The date is present but not dramatized.

Change: Format the date as a mission timestamp at the top of the card:
```
MISSION LOG  //  2024.03.15  //  14:32 UTC
```
This small touch makes the card feel like a classified document. It photographs well and reads clearly at any size.

### B. Add ONE headline or stat as the "hook line"

Do not show all the cosmic data on the card itself. Pick the single most dramatic event and feature it prominently:
```
WHILE A 6.1 MAGNITUDE EARTHQUAKE SHOOK MOROCCO
AND THE ISS PASSED 254 MILES ABOVE YOUR HEAD

YOU DECIDED TO QUIT YOUR JOB.

                    [ DECIDED ]
```

The contrast between cosmic scale and personal decision is what makes this card electric. Less data on the card = more reason to click through to the full briefing on the site.

### C. Add a subtle "briefing number" (uniqueness signal)

```
BRIEFING #00004,271
```

A sequential number at the bottom-left does two things:
1. Makes the card feel like a real document (collectible, unique)
2. Shows social proof -- "thousands of people have generated these"

### D. Platform-specific aspect ratio considerations

The 1080x1350 (4:5) works for Instagram feed. But also consider:
- **Instagram Stories / TikTok**: 1080x1920 (9:16) -- add more vertical breathing room, bigger text
- **Twitter/X**: 1200x675 (roughly 16:9) -- crop tighter, ensure text is legible at thumbnail size
- **iMessage preview**: The OG image is what shows -- make sure the most dramatic line is visible at 300px wide

Quick win: Generate TWO card versions. The default 4:5 for download, and auto-detect when "Share to Stories" is tapped to serve the 9:16 version.

### E. The "DECIDED" stamp treatment

Make the stamp feel more physical. A slight rotation (2-3 degrees), a subtle texture overlay, maybe a faint red or amber color instead of pure white. This makes it look like a real declassified document stamp. People screenshot things that look like artifacts, not things that look like app output.

## 3. THE SHARE TEXT (5 Options)

The goal: create curiosity in the VIEWER. The viewer should think "I need to know what happened on MY day" -- not just "cool card."

### Option 1 (The Revelation)
```
I had no idea what was happening in the world the day I [decided to move to Berlin].

Turns out: a lot.

whileyouweredeciding.com
```
**Why it works**: "I had no idea" + "Turns out: a lot" is a curiosity gap. The viewer immediately wonders what happened on THEIR big day.

### Option 2 (The Scale Play)
```
While I was deciding to [quit my job], 3 earthquakes hit, the ISS orbited Earth 4 times, and an asteroid flew past closer than the Moon.

The universe does not wait.

whileyouweredeciding.com
```
**Why it works**: The specific list creates awe. "The universe does not wait" is quotable. Viewers want their own list.

### Option 3 (The Minimalist)
```
March 15, 2024: The day I [said yes].

Here is what else happened.

whileyouweredeciding.com
```
**Why it works**: Maximum curiosity gap. "Here is what else happened" is an open loop that demands clicking. Works especially well on Twitter/X where brevity wins.

### Option 4 (The Challenge)
```
Look up the day you made your biggest decision. I dare you.

Mine was [March 15, 2024]. The briefing is... a lot.

whileyouweredeciding.com
```
**Why it works**: Direct challenge format. "I dare you" is inherently social. "The briefing is... a lot" is an open loop.

### Option 5 (The Reframe)
```
Every big decision has a backdrop you never saw.

Mine had [a 6.1 earthquake and 2 near-Earth asteroids].

Get your briefing: whileyouweredeciding.com
```
**Why it works**: "A backdrop you never saw" reframes every viewer's life decisions as having a hidden story. Universal applicability = maximum generation loop.

### RECOMMENDED: Option 1 or Option 3 for general use. Option 4 for Twitter/X specifically (the dare format performs well there).

Pre-populate these as share text. Let the user pick or edit, but default to one of these -- do not leave the share text blank or generic.

## 4. THE "GENERATE YOURS" LOOP

This is the most critical growth mechanic. When someone sees a shared card, they need to visit the site. Here is how.

### What the card must communicate (without being an ad)

1. **The URL must be on every card**: `whileyouweredeciding.com` -- subtle but legible
2. **The card must look EASY to make**: If it looks like it took effort, people will not bother. The clean, generated look signals "I just typed something and got this"
3. **The card must tease more content**: The card shows 1-2 events. The full briefing on the site shows 10+. The card is the trailer; the site is the movie.

### The landing page for shared links

When someone clicks a shared link, they should NOT land on the sharer's briefing. They should land on the input form with a single line above it:

```
[Friend's name] got their briefing. Get yours.

[ What did you decide? ]
[ When? ]
[ Generate My Briefing ]
```

This is the Wordle mechanic. You see someone's result, you want YOUR result. Do not show them someone else's -- that satisfies the curiosity and kills the generation loop.

### The 30-second promise

The page should communicate: "This takes 30 seconds." No signup. No email. No friction. Type two things, get your card. The speed of Wordle is what made it viral -- low commitment, high reward.

### Optional: "X briefings generated today" counter

A live counter on the landing page ("4,271 briefings generated today") creates social proof and urgency. Even if the number is small at launch, it establishes the mechanic for when traffic spikes.

## 5. PLATFORM-SPECIFIC OPTIMIZATIONS (Quick Wins)

### Instagram Stories (1-2 hours dev)
- **9:16 card variant**: Reformat the card for vertical full-screen. Same content, more breathing room. Add a "Swipe up" or "Link" call-to-action zone at the bottom.
- **"Add Yours" sticker bait**: Design the card so it naturally invites the Instagram "Add Yours" sticker chain. The implicit challenge: "Post the day of YOUR biggest decision." This is zero-dev on your end -- it just requires the card to look like other "Add Yours" content.
- **Background color**: The dark card on a dark Stories background looks seamless and premium. Do not add a colored border.

### Twitter/X (1 hour dev)
- **Auto-generate tweet text**: When user clicks "Share to X", pre-populate with Option 3 or 4 from above. Twitter's API allows pre-filled tweet text via URL parameters.
- **Card preview**: The OG image must show the decision text clearly at Twitter's small card size. Test at 300x157px -- if you cannot read the decision, increase the font size on the OG-specific image.
- **Thread potential**: Add a "Reply with your briefing" call-to-action in the pre-filled tweet. Threads drive impressions on X.

### TikTok (30 minutes dev, 1 hour content)
- **"Get Ready With Me" format**: The real TikTok play is not the card -- it is a screen recording of someone typing their decision and watching the briefing generate. Add a subtle loading animation between input and result (even if the API is fast) to create a "reveal moment" worth recording.
- **Sound**: If there is any loading/generation animation, add a subtle UI sound effect. TikTok is audio-first. A satisfying "declassified document" sound would get attention.

### iMessage / WhatsApp (1 hour dev)
- **Dynamic OG tags**: This is the single highest-impact change. When someone shares a link, the preview should show THEIR card, not a generic preview. Use dynamic OG meta tags based on a URL parameter (e.g., `whileyouweredeciding.com/b/abc123`).
- **This alone could double share-through rate.** A personalized preview in iMessage is dramatically more likely to get tapped than a generic one.

### Priority ranking by impact-to-effort:
1. Dynamic OG tags (HIGH impact, 1-2 hours) -- do this first
2. Pre-populated share text (HIGH impact, 30 min)
3. 9:16 Stories variant (MEDIUM impact, 1-2 hours)
4. Twitter pre-filled tweet (MEDIUM impact, 30 min)
5. Loading reveal animation for TikTok (LOW-MEDIUM impact, 1 hour)

## 6. THE SOCIAL/COMPARISON MECHANIC (Low Complexity)

### The "Same Day" Discovery

When a user generates a briefing, show them:

```
247 other people also made a big decision on March 15, 2024.
```

This is a simple counter per date. No user accounts, no data sharing, no complexity. Just a number. It makes the user feel part of something, and it is one database query.

### The "Compare Days" Link

After generating your briefing, offer a simple comparison:

```
"Compare with a friend's date"
[ Enter their date ] [ Compare ]
```

This generates a side-by-side: "On YOUR day, 3 earthquakes. On THEIR day, a solar flare." This is shareable as a PAIR -- "My friend and I both quit our jobs. Here is what the universe was doing for each of us."

Implementation: This is just two API calls rendered side-by-side. No social graph, no accounts, no friend connections. Pure stateless comparison. Under 2 hours.

### The "Decision Calendar" (Stretch Goal)

An aggregate, anonymized heatmap: "What days did the most people make big decisions?" This is interesting content on its own and gives the site something worth revisiting. But this is a post-launch feature -- do not build it in the first sprint.

### What NOT to build:
- User accounts or profiles
- Friend lists or social graphs
- Comments or reactions
- Leaderboards

These add complexity without proportional virality. The beauty of this app is its statelessness. One input, one output, one share. Keep it that way.

## THE CORE INSIGHT

The card is currently "look what happened on this day" -- which is trivia.

It needs to become "look what the universe was doing while I changed my life" -- which is identity.

That reframe is the single most important change. Every design tweak, share text option, and platform optimization above serves that reframe. The user is the protagonist. The cosmos is the stage. The card is the proof.

Make the sharer feel like the main character, and they will share it without being asked.
